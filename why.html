Learning a second language is good childhood mind medicine, studies find

May 12, 2009, Susan S. Lang, Cornell University

Teaching young children how to speak a second language is good for their minds, reported two Cornell linguistic researchers.

Learning a second language does not cause language confusion,language delay or cognitive deficit, which have been concerns in the past. In fact, according to studies at the Cornell Language Acquisition Lab (CLAL), children who learn a second language can maintain attention despite external stimuli better than children who know only one language.
